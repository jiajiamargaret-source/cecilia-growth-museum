The Challenge

Build a cardboard boat that could carry people across the pool.

The goal was simple: design, build, and race a boat using cardboard, tape, and paint. Cecilia wanted the boat to do more than float. She wanted it to carry a message about protecting Earth.

Design Process

Sketch. Prototype. Build. Test.

Blueprint

Planning shape, size, folding lines, and structure.

Measuring

Turning cardboard sheets into a functional boat frame.

Prototype

Testing the bow form before building full scale.

Construction

Sealing, painting, and strengthening the structure.

The Message

Keep the Earth chill, not on the grill.

The boat used mountains, Earth symbols, water colors, and recycled cardboard to communicate an environmental message. It was both a working boat and a moving poster for sustainability.

Details

Reflection

Good design is not only functional. It can also inspire people to care.

This project taught Cecilia that engineering is rarely perfect on the first try. Every sketch, prototype, and adjustment helped improve the final design. The boat had to float, but it also had to tell a story.
